**Argus** is a native app (macOS · Android · Windows) that reaches every `claude`
coding-agent session across all your machines at once — your Mac, SLURM/HPC clusters,
Windows boxes, your phone — over [Tailscale](https://tailscale.com), peer-to-peer, with
**no central server**.

Beyond terminals it's a cross-host **file explorer + editor** and a **port-forward hub**:
one calm pane of glass over a sprawl of agents and compute.

<Callout title="Formerly universal_tmux">
Argus is named for **Argus Panoptes**, the hundred-eyed giant of Greek myth who watched over
everything. The `ut` CLI and the per-host broker keep the old `universal_tmux` name
internally — it's load-bearing for tailnet discovery.
</Callout>

## Why it exists

Coding agents now run *everywhere* — a few on your laptop, a dozen on a cluster behind SLURM,
some on a Windows box, maybe one on your phone. Checking on them means a drawer full of SSH
sessions, port-forwards, and `tmux attach`, each tied to one host.

Argus collapses all of that into a single client that sees **every session on every machine**
and tells you which agents are **working**, **waiting on you**, or **idle** — so your
attention goes where it's needed instead of hunting for it. It does this without a backend:
each host runs a small broker, and the app dials those brokers directly over your tailnet.
