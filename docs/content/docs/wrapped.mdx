title: Argus Wrapped
description: A living story and dashboard of how you worked with your agent fleet, computed from your local Activity Journal.
---

Argus Wrapped turns the [Activity Journal](/docs/journal) into a visual recap of your time with the
fleet. It is closer to a living operations review than a usage counter: when you worked, how much
you delegated, which agents and machines carried the load, what shipped, where you intervened, and
how the fleet spent its time.

Open the standalone window with `⇧⌘E` or choose **Argus Wrapped** from the command palette.

## Two ways to read it

- **Story deck** walks through the period as a sequence of full-window cards with keyboard, click,
  and arrow navigation.
- **Overview** places the same evidence in a compact dashboard for scanning and comparison.

Wrapped adapts to the history that exists. Open it after a day and it describes that day; open it
after months and it spans the whole recorded period. Empty or inapplicable cards are omitted instead
of padded with invented content.

## What it derives

Every number comes from journal events:

- active days, time-of-day rhythm, day/hour heatmap, streak, and activity pulse;
- agents, machines, session births and retirements, and the fleet's working/waiting/stuck/idle mix;
- messages you actually sent, phone participation, watch time, and manual corrections;
- delegation ratio—estimated agent working time versus your active intervention time;
- projects, experiments, W&amp;B runs, Git reviews and questions, workflows, todos, and file saves;
- milestones reported by the fleet, leaderboards, superlatives, and earned badges.

Message counts are deliberately de-noised. Empty keypresses, app slash commands, cumulative typing
prefixes, and exact duplicates are excluded so “messages you sent” means messages rather than raw
input events.

## Persona finale

The final card names the working style suggested by the real statistics. Argus sends a compact
numeric digest—not the raw journal or terminal screens—to a local `claude` CLI using Haiku. The
result is cached for that journal window. If the CLI is unavailable or the call fails, Wrapped uses
an immediate rule-based fallback, so the recap itself never depends on a model.

## Privacy and performance

The dashboard computation is a pure local reduction over
`~/Library/Application Support/Argus/journal/*.jsonl`. It runs off the main thread, and the deck
is a bundled offline web view. Only the optional persona digest leaves that local computation
through your configured Claude CLI; the raw event stream remains on disk.

Wrapped is currently a macOS feature because the canonical journal is assembled on the Mac. Phone
messages still contribute: Android forwards its sent utterances to the Mac journal through the
append-only sync inbox.

<Callout type="info">
Wrapped can only describe the period recorded by the Activity Journal. Turning journal capture off
also pauses the evidence available to future recaps.
</Callout>
